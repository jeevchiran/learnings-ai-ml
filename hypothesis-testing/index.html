Hypothesis Testing: From Intuition to Inference

A rigorous, interactive path through statistical hypothesis testing. Every concept is grounded in a real-world problem before any formulas appear. Each module follows a Why → What → How arc.

Learning Path

Study the modules in sequence. Each builds directly on the previous one. By the end you will be able to design, execute, and interpret any of the core parametric tests used in industry and research.

  • Module 1: Foundations of Hypothesis Testing
    The logic of statistical inference. Null and alternative hypotheses, the decision framework, one-tailed vs two-tailed tests, and what a test statistic actually measures.
  • Module 2: Errors, Alpha, p-value, and the Critical Value Method
    Type I and Type II errors, the significance level alpha, statistical power, the p-value (what it is and what it is not), and the critical value approach. Both methods always agree.
  • Module 3: One-Sample Z-Test
    Testing a single population mean when sigma is known. Full derivation from the sampling distribution of the mean, step-by-step procedure, confidence intervals, and the effect of sample size on power.
  • Module 4: Two-Sample Tests — Z-Test, t-Test, and A/B Testing
    Comparing two population means. Two-population Z-test, Welch's two-sample t-test, and A/B testing as a direct application. Effect size (Cohen's d) and practical vs statistical significance.
  • Module 5: Paired t-Test
    Dependent samples and before-after designs. Why pairing eliminates between-subject noise, reduction to a one-sample t-test on differences, and when to use paired vs independent tests.
