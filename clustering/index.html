K-Means Clustering

A rigorous, interactive path through unsupervised learning with K-means. Starting from why clustering is needed, building up the mathematics of distance and centroids, then deriving the algorithm and its evaluation metrics. Each module follows a Why → What → How arc with interactive widgets and practice problems.

Learning Path

  • Module 1: Why Clustering? Intuition and the K-Means Idea
    The unsupervised learning setting, what clustering formalises, real-world applications, and a first look at K-means as a partitioning algorithm. Interactive animated K-means demo.
  • Module 2: Distance, Means, and Centroids
    Euclidean distance derived from Pythagoras, Manhattan and cosine similarity, the centroid as the point minimising squared distances, and why Euclidean distance + arithmetic mean are mathematically inseparable in K-means.
  • Module 3: Inertia (WCSS) and the Elbow Method
    Within-Cluster Sum of Squares derived as the K-means objective, monotone decrease with K, the Elbow Method, K-means as coordinate descent on WCSS, and the gap statistic.
  • Module 4: Silhouette Score and Evaluation Metrics
    Silhouette score derived from intra- and inter-cluster distances, the [-1,+1] range interpretation, silhouette plots, Davies-Bouldin index, Calinski-Harabasz index, and a practical guide to choosing K.
